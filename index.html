What insurance should a Greenville small business review before signing a lease or contract?

Business insurance questions usually become urgent when a landlord, client, lender, or vendor asks for proof of coverage. This checklist helps organize that review before the deadline.

Short answer: Before signing, Greenville businesses should review certificate wording, general liability, business property, hired and non-owned auto, workers compensation, cyber exposure, and any contract-specific insurance requirements.

What certificate of insurance wording should a Greenville business check?

Look for required limits, additional insured wording, waiver of subrogation, primary and noncontributory language, and the exact certificate holder name and address.

When does a small business need more than general liability?

General liability is important, but it may not cover owned vehicles, employee injuries, professional mistakes, cyber events, or property losses. The business activity controls the coverage mix.

How do lease requirements affect business insurance?

Commercial leases often require liability limits, property coverage for tenant improvements, and proof that the landlord is listed correctly on the certificate.

What should a Greenville business prepare before requesting quotes?

Gather payroll, revenue, locations, operations, vehicle use, subcontractor details, prior claims, and copies of contracts or leases with insurance requirements.

Common follow-up questions about this Greenville insurance topic

Is a certificate of insurance the same as a policy?

No. A certificate is proof of coverage. The policy controls what is actually covered.

Can one business policy cover every risk?

Usually no. Many businesses need a package of coverages matched to operations and contracts.
